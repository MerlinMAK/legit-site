The problem

Right now, your agent is a stranger on the internet.

It has no identity, no standing, and no one to answer for it. That breaks in three expensive ways.

01

It gets treated like a bot

Sites can't tell your agent from a malicious scraper, so it's blocked, rate-limited, fed CAPTCHAs, or served garbage. The open web is closing to anonymous machines.

02

Its actions land on you

Every page it touches, every term it brushes, every action it takes traces back to your personal name — not a contained entity. You're carrying liability you never named.

03

No one can trust it — or stop it

There's no way to prove who stands behind it, show it's in good standing, or revoke it cleanly. Without accountability, there's no trust. Without trust, no access.

Built on the standard

Your agent, signed and recognized.

Web Bot Auth is the emerging way the internet tells good agents from bad ones — cryptographically signed requests that prove who's behind the traffic. Legit issues and manages that identity for you, so your agent is recognized wherever the standard is honored. It's built on the IETF HTTP Message Signatures standard (RFC 9421) — recognition depends on each platform's policy, so it's standing, not a guarantee. Available as an MCP server your agent can call to generate valid headers on the fly.

  • → Honored by Cloudflare, AWS, Vercel, Shopify, Akamai
