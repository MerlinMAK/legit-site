The honest part

What we'd rather tell you up front.

Not every project needs an entity. If you're not earning, signing, or carrying liability yet, a free key-bound company is plenty — form a real one when a bank, client, or contract actually asks.

Free means pseudonymous to start. Your free entity is bound to a key, not a face. We don't ask for a selfie until the EIN step, where a real bank account legally needs a verified human — and even then your identity is shielded from the public and disclosed only on valid legal process. One free entity per verified person, so this doesn't become a shell-company mill.

California residents — the $800 caveat. California's $800/yr minimum franchise tax can apply to an entity it considers to be doing business in California, which it reads to include an entity managed by a CA resident — even with no activity, and per entity. That's a state tax you'd owe California directly, not a Legit fee; a free entity doesn't avoid it if you operate from CA. We flag it before you form. Not tax advice.

We're not a law or accounting firm, and an agent's ID badge is recognition, not a guarantee — each platform decides whether to honor it. What we are is the verified record of who's behind your company and what you authorized.
